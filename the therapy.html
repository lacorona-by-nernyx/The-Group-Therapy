Whispered Fragments

Verse-born confessions lingering between shadow and light

Melancholy

The Weight of Absence

Your ghost sits at my table, drinks from my favorite cup. The steam rises between us— a veil too thin to touch, too thick to see through. I set a place for you still, though the tea grows cold, and the biscuits harden like my heart trying to forget how to miss you.
Cool

Urban Nocturne

Streetlights hum a lonely tune, against the velvet night. Rain-slicked asphalt reflects the moon, a fractured, pale moonlight. Footsteps echo in the void, a rhythm soft and low. Another story, self-destroyed, in shadows come and go.
Cute

Ephemeral Bloom

A shy bud on a fragile stem, unfurls with morning's grace. A tiny jewel, a diadem, in this forgotten space. Its gentle beauty, brief and bright, a whisper in the air. A fleeting solace in the light, a moment beyond compare.
Happy

Sunlit Meadow

Golden rays on swaying grass, a gentle breeze takes flight. Laughter echoes as clouds pass, a day bathed in warm light. The world breathes easy, calm, and free, a vibrant, joyful hue. A simple, sweet felicity, in everything I view.
Reflection

Echoes in the Glass

The mirror holds a stranger's gaze, familiar, yet unknown. Each wrinkle etched by passing days, a story softly sown. A quiet sigh, a whispered name, reverberates through years. A flicker of an old, dying flame, reflected in soft tears.
Mystery

Whispers of the Crypt

Beneath the ancient, moss-kissed stone, where silence softly sleeps, a forgotten tale, now overgrown, the earth in shadows keeps. The wind, a sigh, a mournful plea, through branches bare and stark. Secrets held for eternity, in the silent, brooding dark.
Longing

Chasing Love

I’ve chased love through endless days, Through fleeting moments, endless maze. Each time I thought I’d found the light, It slipped away, out of my sight. A heart that yearns, a soul that aches, For every love that never takes. I give, I hope, I open wide, Yet love retreats, it cannot hide. The path is long, the night is cold, I seek the warmth, but none to hold. Am I the fool, or is it true, That love’s just something I can’t pursue? Through years I wander, lost in dreams, Each face a shadow, or so it seems. I reach, I fall, I rise again, Yet love remains a distant friend. So I chase, but never find, A love that stays, a peace of mind. And all I’m left with, here alone, A heart that seeks, but never’s home.
Contemplation

love and time

You need to water the flower every day, Or face the lament of watching it fade. Maybe time decides? Or is that just what people say? Only you can choose if time is true— The power to think and act lies deep in you. Be bold when the moment calls your name, Don't let insecurity play its game. It takes two souls to form a home, Time is short—don't walk alone. If he loves your flaws, your spark, your wild, Your laughter, tears, your inner child— He's the one. Pick a man who calls his mother “queen,” For that’s the kind of love he’ll bring. What makes her perfect? Who can tell? Some make lists, some just feel. And that’s okay—life’s not meant to make full sense. If she nurtures like a mother, She truly cares. If he’s childlike in your presence, You are his peace. But love— Love is still the mystery We breathe, not fully understand
Resignation

"Maybe Time Will Say"

Why is love so cruel when it gives back its bruise? Why does pain spread vast when love is refused? Why doesn't truth last, like the sand that slips away, As the hours bleed out into the endless gray? Is life just a game? Or is there more to know? Something deeper beneath this constant woe. I cry and cry, yet no tears fall from my eyes, Perhaps I’m dried inside, or love was just a lie. The sky is blue, the sea is the same, Yet I cannot understand why I can’t win this game. I try, I reach, but I can never pull, And still, I wonder, am I just a fool? Maybe time will speak the words I need to say, Maybe it’s time to let this poem fade away. To bury it deep in a river or lake, And let the silence heal what my heart can't take.
Observation

"Not Every Reaching Hand Wants to Hold You”

He didn’t want her love. Not in the way stories write it, not in the way movies sell it— he wanted something rarer. A friend. A sister in soul, someone to laugh with when the world forgot him, someone to sit in silence without fear of being unseen. But when he reached out, she flinched, as if every word he offered came wrapped in hidden motives. As if being a man meant wanting something he never asked for. She thought he was chasing romance. He wasn’t. He was chasing realness. Kinship. A place where his heart didn’t have to perform. He spoke gently. Not because he was trying to impress— but because he didn’t want to scare her. Because the world already taught her that most men only knock when they want to enter. But he just wanted to sit on the porch of her life and talk like old friends who'd never met before. He watched her back away. Not with cruelty, but with assumption. And he wondered— how many men before him wore friendship as a disguise so now she couldn’t see it when it was real? He didn’t blame her. But the ache stayed. Not because he was rejected, but because she never saw what he was really offering: A hand, without a price. A friendship, without an agenda. A bond, without romance. Not every reaching hand wants to hold you. Some just want to build something that doesn't break when love is taken off the table.
Betrayal

“They Said They Would Stay”

They said they would stay. But they were already halfway out the door when they spoke. Their promises were rehearsed— clean, practiced lines delivered like poetry to distract from the silence behind their eyes. They called it friendship. But what they meant was convenience. Loyalty, until discomfort. Kindness, until effort. Truth, until it didn’t serve them anymore. They praised the soul, but only when it fit their story. When the cracks showed— they called it too much. They smiled at beauty, but never saw beyond the skin. Flattery came easy when they wanted something. Distance came faster when nothing was left to gain. They learned how to mimic care. How to say just enough. How to look like they meant it while measuring their exit. They judged quietly, with polite words and gentle tones. But the betrayal was already packed in the back of their mind. They said they would stay. But they had conditions. Unspoken rules. Fine print. And when reality asked them to show up— they disappeared. This was never about connection. It was performance. A mask for every season. A role played until the applause stopped. They said they would stay. But they never intended to. They just didn’t think anyone would notice they were lying.
Intrigue

“Lab Notes Never Read”

He wrote in ink that disappeared, In journals no one ever cleared. A name—her name—on every page, A ghost within his silent cage. She passed like vapor in the glass, A spark no instrument could pass. And though his lab stayed cold and dim, The strangest formula was him.
Truth

"The Answers No One Wants"

“How do I know it’s love?” You don’t. You guess. And by the time you’re sure, you’re already in too deep to leave clean. “What if I’m not enough?” You won’t be. Not always. Someone else will make them laugh differently, and you’ll feel it. “Why does my chest feel like it’s breaking?” Because part of you knows they could break you, and you're letting them anyway. “What if they leave?” Then they leave. And the world keeps spinning like they were never the center of yours. “Can I show them the parts I hide?” You can. But don’t expect them to stay for all of it. People love the light.
